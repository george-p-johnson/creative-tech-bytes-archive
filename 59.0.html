KICKS VISION EXPERIENCE (APPLE VISION PRO)

George P. Johnson Experience Marketing partnered with Nissan to develop a cutting-edge, immersive experience showcasing the 2025 Nissan Kicks, highlighting its design influences. This was achieved by leveraging Apple Vision Pro (AVP) headsets, the latest in spatial computing technology, to create a personalized and interactive journey for each guest.

The experience took place in a hi-fi lounge setting, designed to be both comfortable and engaging. Guests, guided by dedicated docents, used the AVP headsets to explore a virtual recreation of the space, enriched with interactive elements. By focusing on specific objects marked with floating hotspots, users could delve deeper into the inspiration behind the Kicks' design. This exploration was further enhanced by AI-generated voiceovers providing detailed narration and insights.

From a technological standpoint, the experience showcased the potential of AVP headsets for interactive storytelling and personalized exploration. The use of spatial computing allowed guests to engage with the virtual environment in a natural and intuitive way, while AI-generated narration provided a dynamic and informative layer to the experience. Additionally, the integration of a live stream of the user's perspective on a separate monitor allowed for both docent assistance and a shared experience for other guests in the lounge.

Adobe Max 2024: all the major announcements around design and AI

Adobe's MAX 2024 conference unveiled a host of new AI-powered tools and updates for its Creative Cloud suite. Notably, Adobe announced its first generative AI video model in Premiere Pro, aimed at streamlining video editing tasks. Other key developments include AI enhancements across Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign, and a major update to the Frame.io collaboration platform. Adobe also emphasized a shift from AI simply generating content from prompts to offering more specialized tools that give creators greater control and efficiency.

Mets vs. Dodgers: How Fox Sports is utilizing 41 cameras for NLCS broadcasts

Fox Sports is enhancing its NLCS and World Series coverage with an array of specialty cameras, totaling 41 for the NLCS. These include a Movi camera for player closeups, UmpCam for unique perspectives, FlyCam for dynamic field-level shots, and live drones for aerial footage. While not entirely new, these technologies have been upgraded with better sensors, lenses, and transmission capabilities. Fox Sports emphasizes a collaborative approach to innovation, sharing advancements across different sports and leagues, and prioritizing sustainable practices for repeatable and efficient production.

Google to buy nuclear power for AI datacentres in ‚Äòworld first‚Äô deal

In a groundbreaking move, Google has agreed to purchase 500 megawatts of electricity from Kairos Power's small modular nuclear reactors (SMRs) to power its AI data centers. This deal, the first of its kind, aims to provide a low-carbon energy solution for the increasing power demands of AI technologies. While SMRs offer potential advantages in terms of cost and scalability compared to traditional nuclear plants, they remain a subject of debate regarding their economic viability and environmental impact.
