#TechNews

#AR

Meta Connect 2025: the 6 biggest announcements

At the Meta Connect 2025 event, Meta announced a new line of smart glasses, including the Meta Ray-Ban Display with a built-in screen and a wrist-worn Neural Band for control. The company also unveiled the Ray-Ban Meta (Gen 2) glasses, which feature a longer battery life and an improved camera, and the Oakley Meta Vanguard, designed for athletes with integrations for fitness apps. Beyond hardware, Meta announced new software tools to enhance its metaverse and VR ecosystems. These include Hyperscape Capture for 3D room scanning, Meta Horizon Studio as a new creator hub, and Horizon TV as an entertainment platform for Quest headsets.

#Gaming

Roblox announces new AI tools and short-form video platform for creators

Roblox recently announced two new features at its Developers Conference: a short-form video platform and a suite of AI tools for creators. The video platform, called "Roblox Moments," is a TikTok-like feature that allows users to create and share clips up to 30 seconds long. This move is intended to consolidate the popular Roblox-related video content that currently resides on other platforms like YouTube and TikTok. The new AI tools include the ability to generate in-game items from text prompts and real-time voice chat translation. These announcements were made amid recent scrutiny and lawsuits against the company regarding its child safety features.

#Space

NASA announces "groundbreaking discovery" of life on Mars

NASA recently announced what could be the strongest evidence yet for life on Mars. The discovery is based on a mudstone core sample drilled by the Perseverance rover in July 2024. Scientists found minerals in the sample, specifically vivianite and greigite, that are often associated with microbial activity on Earth. While these findings are consistent with the presence of life, they do not definitively prove it, as nonbiological chemical reactions could also produce the same results. The discovery is considered a "potential biosignature" and will be further analyzed once the sample is returned to Earth.

#App

Google Gemini is the top free iPhone app

Google's Gemini app has recently become the top free app in the US App Store, a position attributed to its Nano Banana image editing model. The app's success has resulted in 23 million new users since its launch in late August, and it also holds the second-place ranking in Canada and the UK. The Nano Banana model's popularity is due to its ability to maintain character likeness in images, with over 500 million images having been edited. The article notes that while the app is free, it provides daily editing limits of 100 images for free users and 1,000 for subscribers. Other Google apps are also listed among the top free apps.

#Coding

OpenAI upgrades Codex with a new version of GPT-5

OpenAI has released a new, specialized version of its language model, GPT-5-Codex, which is optimized for "agentic coding" and software engineering tasks. The new model can dynamically adjust its "thinking time," spending more computational effort on complex problems and less on simple ones. OpenAI reports that the model is capable of operating independently on difficult projects for several hours. This updated version is integrated into tools such as the Codex CLI, IDE extensions, and GitHub for automated code reviews. It is available to users on various paid ChatGPT plans, with plans to make it available via API soon.

#Streaming

Netflix to Add NASA+ as It Launches Into Live Streaming Feeds

NASA is partnering with Netflix to bring its live programming to a broader audience. Starting in the summer, live content from NASA+, including rocket launches, spacewalks, and views from the International Space Station, will become available on the streaming platform. This partnership allows NASA to reach a global audience of more than 700 million Netflix subscribers. While the live content will be available on Netflix, NASA's own ad-free and subscription-free streaming service, NASA+, will continue to be available through its app and website.
